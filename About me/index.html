Hi, I hope you're doing well.

I come from a rural town that sits on the foothills of the Himalayan Mountains in Northern India. I grew up in the farms and led a simple childhood. Looking at the night sky during moonless nights from the fields were one of best days of my childhood.

I have always wanted to study stars and twinkling things. I went to my local high school where I read Math and Science. I was growing and it was my senior year when I had my first crush. She taught me the importance of love and how it matters beyond anything else. I remember writing my first short story about this girl and how my English teacher read that in front of the whole class. Such a wonderful memory!

I went to a university near my hometown and did my bachelor's there. During this time, I experienced pretty much everything; from loosing a loved one to acing several competitions. Life was teaching me and I kept on learning. But my love for the shimmering night sky never faded. I applied for the master's program at UMass Dartmouth, got accepted and came to this foreign land, thousands of miles from family and friends. It was hard leaving the place I grew up. But then, we all have to leave our comfort zones and work hard on achieving our dreams. So here I am now, studying stars and living the best in me everyday.

I make mistakes. I get angry sometimes. I have lost friends. I have lost love. So, you see my journey has been an ordinary one. It is very humbling when you realize that you are just someone. One of the very best things that I have done is to fall in love with myself. I accept my deficits and always try to build from that, one little step at a time. I am not perfect and will never be.
