Research

My research focuses on stellar physics and stellar evolution. Mostly, I am developing magnetohydrodynamics(MHD) solvers to build numerical solutions for SN Ia. MHD plays an important role in the long term evolution of white dwarf mergers and can lead us to better understand their progenitor systems.

My current effort involves a direct comparision of the fully MHD solution the white dwarf merger versus that obtained for an alpha disk hydrodynamic treatment. Much of the current literature is still based upon the latter, in which the differential rotation in the tidally disrupted disk leads to a hot inflated envelope in the white dwarf merger, which expands dramatically to non-degenerate stellar radii, in strong contrast to the MHD merger which remains compact.

I earned my bachelor's in Aerospace Engineering from UPES where my advisor was Ugur Guven. My undergraduate thesis concerned the orbital trajectories of objects under influence of a gravitational source. I built on transfer algorithms like Hohmann and modified them using RK4 scheme for interpolation. I also extended the solver to include effects like orbital decay, albedo and third body perturbations. I am particularly proud of my work because it went on to influence a bunch of other computer science students who then added elements of neural network to prepare an orbital collision avoidance system.
