Research

I am deeply passionate about computation, scientific analysis, and solving complex problems. My research background has equipped me with a solid foundation in data analysis, algorithm development, and computational techniques that I am eager to apply in a professional setting to make a meaningful impact.

My work in developing a novel Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) solver for White Dwarf mergers showcases my ability to adapt and optimize algorithms for high-performance computing. I have successfully integrated computational physics, machine learning, and data processing techniques to understand the underlying processes and structures of these celestial events.

As I continue to refine the MHD solver, my focus lies in comparing the results with traditional hydrodynamic models and uncovering the distinct characteristics of fully magnetized mergers. My expertise in algorithm development, software optimization, and data analysis positions me to excel in roles that leverage cutting-edge technology to drive scientific discovery and innovation.

I am also interested in solving astrophysical problems using machine learning. I am developing and and training a Convolutional Neural Network on supernova spectra data from Berkeley SN Ia Program. The main objective is to reduce the supernova classification time from hours (template matching methods) to seconds without compromising on accuracy. I have also implemented segmentation methods that allow for better feature recognition.

I earned my bachelor's in Aerospace Engineering from UPES where my advisor was Dr Ugur Guven. My undergraduate thesis concerned the N-body simulations using Monte Carlo methods. I also worked on building orbital transfer algorithms (Hohmann) and modified them using RK4 scheme for interpolation. I created a machine learning algorithm that trained itself in real-time on data produced by the orbital analysis module which led to a ML based collision avoidance system. I also extended the solver to include effects like orbital decay, albedo and third body perturbations. I am particularly proud of my work because it went on to influence a bunch of other computer science students who then added elements of neural network in the solver and developed an orbital collision avoidance system.
